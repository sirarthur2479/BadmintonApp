# BadmintonApp — User Guide

A personal training companion for badminton players. Track every session, review your progress, study techniques, and plan for matches — all in one place.

---

## Navigation

The app has four tabs at the bottom:

| Tab | Icon | What it's for |
|-----|------|--------------|
| **Home** | House | Today's snapshot — streak, weekly stats, last session |
| **Train** | Dumbbell | Log sessions, view training history |
| **Learn** | Open book | Technique library with tutorials |
| **Profile** | Person | Your player info, goals, progress charts, tournament records |

---

## Home

The Home tab shows your training at a glance:

- **Training streak** — how many days in a row you've trained
- **This week / this month** — session counts
- **Last session** — drills practiced, goal, and how well you achieved it

Tap the **+** button to log a new training session immediately.

---

## Logging a Training Session

Go to **Train → tap the + button**.

Fill in:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Date** | Defaults to today; tap to change |
| **Session Goal** | What you want to achieve in this session (e.g. "Improve net kill consistency") |
| **Drills** | Tap the drills you practiced — select as many as apply |
| **Custom drill** | Tap **+ New tag** to add a drill that isn't in the list; long-press to delete |
| **Reflection** | Answer 6 short questions after the session to lock in learning |
| **Goal Achievement** | Rate 1–5 stars: how close did you get to your goal? |
| **Player remarks** | What you felt went well or was hard |
| **Coach remarks** | Notes from your coach (if applicable) |
| **Duration** | Session length in minutes (in Advanced section) |
| **Photo** | Attach a photo from your gallery |

Tap **Save** when done.

> **Tip:** The 6 reflection questions are designed to build training awareness over time. Even 1–2 sentence answers are enough.

### Editing a past session

Open **Train** (history list), tap the **edit icon** on any session card, make changes, and tap **Update**.

---

## Training History

The **Train** tab shows all sessions, newest first.

- Each card shows the date, goal, drills practiced, goal achievement stars, and your remarks
- **Swipe left** on a card to delete it (confirms before deleting)
- Tap the **edit icon** to re-open and update a session

## Logging a Match

Go to **Train → Match Log → tap the + button**.

The form is split into three sections: **Pre-Match**, **Post-Match**, and **Videos**.

### Pre-Match

Fill these in before (or just after) the match starts:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Date** | Defaults to today; tap to change |
| **Opponent name** | Who you're playing |
| **Opponent notes** | Playing style, known strengths/weaknesses |
| **Game plan** | Your intended strategy for this match |
| **Targeted areas** | Specific weaknesses or court zones to exploit |
| **Mental readiness** | Rate 1–5: how focused and calm are you? |
| **Physical readiness** | Rate 1–5: how fresh and warmed up are you? |

### Post-Match

Complete this section after the match:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Score** | Final score (e.g. "21-18, 15-21, 21-19") |
| **Overall performance** | Rate 1–5: how well did you play overall? |
| **Move performances** | Rate each of the 8 key moves 1–5 and add a short note |
| **Strategy worked?** | Yes / Partially / No |
| **Strategy review** | What from the game plan worked and what didn't |
| **Key turning points** | Moments that changed the momentum |
| **Critical review** | Honest self-assessment — what to improve |
| **Next focus** | The one thing to work on in the next training session |

**The 8 key moves rated in every match:**

| Move | What to assess |
|------|---------------|
| Service | Accuracy, variation, pressure applied |
| Return of Service | Aggressiveness, placement |
| Net Play | Net kills, net lifts, deception |
| Smash | Power, placement, frequency of use |
| Drop Shot | Tightness to the net, disguise |
| Clear / Lift | Height, depth, recovery after |
| Defense | Reach, stability, quality of return |
| Footwork | Speed to the shuttle, balance, recovery |

### Videos

Attach reference videos for post-match review:

- Tap **+ Add video** → enter a label and paste a URL or enter a local file path
- Videos are saved alongside the match log for later viewing

### Editing a past match log

Open **Train → Match Log**, tap the **edit icon** on any match card, make changes, and tap **Update**.

### Deleting a match log

**Swipe left** on a match card and confirm deletion.

## Technique Library

Go to the **Learn** tab.

### Browsing techniques

Use the category filter chips at the top to narrow by:
- **Footwork** — Split Step, Shadow, Lunge, Recovery
- **Stroke** — Smash, Drop Shot, Clear, Net Kill, Net Lift
- **Serve** — Low Serve, Flick Serve, Drive Serve
- **Tactics** — Cross-Court Play, Deception, Court Coverage

### Reading a technique

Tap any technique card to open the detail view:

1. **Tutorial video** — plays inline (YouTube). If no video is assigned yet, shows a placeholder
2. **Open in YouTube** — small link below the video to open it in the YouTube app
3. **Skill level selector** — switch between **Beginner**, **Intermediate**, and **Advanced** — the tips and common mistakes update accordingly
4. **Overview** — description of the technique
5. **Key Tips** — what to focus on
6. **Common Mistakes** — what to avoid
7. **Related Drills** — drills to practice this technique in training

> **Tip:** Before a training session, open the technique you're focusing on and read through the tips. After the session, come back and check the common mistakes to self-diagnose.

## Progress & Charts

Go to **Profile → Progress & Charts**.

- **Total sessions** and **best streak** summary
- **Sessions per week** bar chart — 8-week rolling view
- **Average intensity per week** line chart — shows training load trend

Use these charts to check if your training volume is consistent and whether you're increasing or easing off load before a competition.

---

## Tournament Tracker

Go to **Profile → Tournament History**.

### Adding a tournament

Tap **+** → fill in name, location, format, and date → **Add**.

### Logging match results

Expand a tournament → tap **Add Match** → enter opponent name, score, and win/loss.

### Stats shown

- Per-tournament: wins, losses
- Overall: total wins, total losses, **win rate %**

## Player Profile

Go to the **Profile** tab and tap **Edit**.

| Field | Notes |
|-------|-------|
| Name | Your display name |
| Club | Your club or school |
| Age / Years playing | Used for context in training recommendations |
| Playing style | Attacking / Defensive / All-round |
| Preferred grip | Forehand / Backhand |
| Short-term goal | What you're working toward in the next few weeks |
| Long-term goal | Your bigger ambition (e.g. "Win district championship") |
| Photo | Tap the avatar to change your profile picture |

Tap **Save** to confirm changes.

## Multiple Players (Web only)

The web version supports an **Account → Players** model, ideal for families:

- One account (email + password) can have multiple players (e.g. parent + two children)
- Each player has completely separate training data (sessions, matches, tournaments)
- Tap a player's card on the Player Select screen to switch active player
- A player switcher button in the app lets you change players without logging out

## Tips for Getting the Most Out of the App

**For a young player (self-training):**
- Set a specific goal before every session — even "practice 3 different serves" counts
- Rate your goal achievement honestly after each session — it reveals patterns over time
- Check the Technique Library before working on a new skill, then watch the video after your session to compare your form

**For a player with a coach:**
- Fill in **Coach Remarks** after each session while the feedback is fresh
- Use the **Reflection questions** as a pre-session and post-session checklist
- Show your coach the training history and charts at the start of each lesson

**Before a match:**
- Use the Match Log's Pre-Match section to write your game plan and rate your readiness
- Review your last 2–3 sessions to identify what you've been working on and plan to use it

**After a match:**
- Complete the Post-Match section while the match is still fresh (same day)
- Rate each of the 8 key moves and write a short critical review
- Set **Next Focus** — this becomes the goal for your next training session
